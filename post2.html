Recognizing Phishing Scams

Phishing attacks are designed to trick you into giving up sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details. This post helps you recognize the red flags and stay protected.

1. Watch Out for Urgent or Threatening Language

Emails that say "Your account will be closed!" or "Immediate action required!" are often phishing attempts trying to pressure you into acting quickly.

2. Double-Check the Sender's Email Address

Phishers often use addresses that look similar to real ones, like support@paypa1.com (notice the "1" instead of "l").

3. Don’t Click on Suspicious Links

Hover over a link to preview the URL. If it doesn’t match the official website or looks strange, don’t click it.

4. Be Careful with Attachments

Never download attachments from unknown or unverified senders—they may contain malware or ransomware.

5. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

Even if you fall for a phishing attack, MFA can stop the attacker from accessing your account with just your password.
