Two-Factor Authentication: Why You Need It

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of protection to your online accounts. It ensures that even if someone gets your password, they still can’t log in without a second factor.

What Is Two-Factor Authentication?

2FA is a security process where users provide two different authentication factors to verify themselves. It's a combination of something you know (like a password) and something you have (like your phone).

Why Is It Important?

Even strong passwords can be cracked or stolen. 2FA makes it significantly harder for attackers to gain access to your account, adding a second hurdle they must clear.

Common Forms of 2FA

  • Text message codes
  • Authenticator apps like Google Authenticator or Authy
  • Biometrics such as fingerprint or facial recognition

Set It Up Now

Most major services like Google, Facebook, and banking apps support 2FA. Go into your account settings and enable it—your future self will thank you.
