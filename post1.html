Top 5 Cyber Hygiene Tips

Cyber hygiene is the practice of maintaining your digital systems and habits in a clean, secure, and healthy state. Just like personal hygiene, regular attention to your cyber habits keeps your data and devices protected from threats. Here are the top 5 cyber hygiene tips everyone should follow:

1. Use Strong and Unique Passwords

Never reuse passwords across platforms. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and special characters. Consider using a password manager to help you manage and generate strong passwords.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step — like a code sent to your phone — even if someone gets your password.

3. Keep Software and Systems Updated

Software updates often contain patches for security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates whenever possible to keep your system secure without having to think about it.

4. Be Aware of Phishing Attacks

Phishing emails and websites trick you into giving away sensitive information. Always verify the source of an email or link, and avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.

5. Back Up Your Data Regularly

Whether it's cloud storage or an external hard drive, keeping backups ensures you don’t lose your data in case of a ransomware attack, system failure, or accidental deletion.

By incorporating these habits into your daily routine, you'll be well on your way to protecting your personal and professional digital presence. Stay safe out there!
